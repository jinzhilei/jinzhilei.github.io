## Welcome to the Lei Lab

We are a computational and mathematical biology research group at the [School of Mathematical Sciences](https://sxkxxy.tiangong.edu.cn/), [Tiangong University](https://www.tiangong.edu.cn/). Our mission is to understand the quantitative principles governing complex biological systems—from individual cells to cancer evolution—through the lens of mathematics, dynamical systems, and computation.

We develop mathematical models and computational frameworks to decode how cells make decisions, how tissues maintain homeostasis, and how dysregulation leads to cancer. A central theme of our work is uncovering the interplay between genetic circuits and epigenetic landscapes in cell fate transitions.

---

### Research Interests

- **Differential Equations:** theory and methods for integrating ordinary differential equations; differential Galois theory
- **Dynamical Systems:** Hamiltonian systems, Morse's twist theory and applications
- **Mathematical Biology:** protein folding mechanisms; systems biology modeling of gene regulatory networks in diseases such as hematological disorders and cancer
- **Cancer Systems Biology:** mechanisms of cancer development; computational cancer biology; mathematical oncology

### Principal Investigator

**Prof. Jinzhi Lei (雷锦志)** is a Professor, doctoral supervisor, and Distinguished Professor of Tianjin (天津市特聘教授) at Tiangong University (2020–present).

**Education:**
- Ph.D. in Analytical Mechanics, Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (Beihang University), 1998–2001. Thesis: *Double Stars System and Correlative Mathematical Aspects*
- M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics, Beihang University, 1995–1998. Thesis: *Application of Lie group for integrability of differential equation*
- B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics, Beihang University, 1991–1995

**Professional Experience:**
- 2020–present: Professor, School of Mathematical Sciences, Tiangong University
- 2006–2019: Associate Researcher, Zhou Pei-Yuan Center for Applied Mathematics, Tsinghua University
- 2003–2006: Research Assistant, Zhou Pei-Yuan Center for Applied Mathematics, Tsinghua University
- 2001–2003: Postdoctoral Fellow, Department of Mathematical Science, Tsinghua University

**Selected Visiting Positions:**
- 2015: Visiting Scholar, Center for Complex Biological Systems, UC Irvine
- 2013: Visiting Scholar, KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden (Erasmus Mundus)
- 2012: Visiting Scholar, CAMBAM, McGill University, Canada
- 2008–2009, 2004–2005: Visiting Scholar / Visiting Assistant Professor, Department of Mathematics, UC Irvine
- 2005: Exchange Scholar, Centre for Nonlinear Dynamics in Physiology and Medicine, McGill University

He has published over 90 peer-reviewed research articles, authored and co-authored five monographs on systems biology, and attracted over 17,000 citations (Google Scholar). He serves on the editorial boards of *Mathematical Biosciences and Engineering* and *Mathematics in Applied Sciences and Engineering*, among others.
